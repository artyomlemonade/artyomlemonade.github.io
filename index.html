How has IT affected positively the nature ?

1. Decreased use of paper. It was widespread to use paper documentation on daily-life basis 20-30 years ago. However, nowadays advanced technologies had facilitated that process. Therefore, the most amount of information is stored in memory storages. Those memory storages secures such data as: WWW resources, police data base, financial spreadsheets and etc.

2. Tools which helps environment. Environmental monitoring and management are one of the sectors that have been transformed by the quick development of IT. We can now gather, evaluate, and interpret enormous volumes of environmental data thanks to IT, which has made it possible for us to make more informed decisions about environmental protection.

3. Developing new methods in the sphere of collaboration and transportation. IT applications have affected on the way of communication between people. Before the occurrence of advanced IT devices, people need to travel a huge amount of kilometers in order to talk with their families in friends. On the contrast, now there are a lot of IT apps such as video conferencing, instant messaging, and collaboration software, which have enabled people to work and communicate effectively without having to travel frequently.

4. Assistance in the production and saving of raw materials. New IT technologies allow us to save materials during the production and processing , what will help to significantly reduce deforestation, senseless waste of resources . In fact , previous century people don’t even look at waste , we have spent resources without thinking about the consequences, which led us to reduce the ozone layer, global warming, atmospheric pollution, greenhouse effect, ocean pollution, reduction of biodiversity and that's not all .

5. IT, in particular, robots have assisted people with farming. They have a plenty of advantages that we’ll discuss now. 1. Increased Efficiency: Robots can work around the clock without getting tired, which can significantly increase the efficiency of farming operations. 2. Precision Farming: Robots can be equipped with sensors and cameras that allow them to perform tasks with a high degree of precision. This can lead to better crop yields and less waste. 3. Reduced Labor Costs: Farming is hard work, and it can be difficult to find enough labor, especially during peak times like planting and harvest seasons.

Negative impact

On the other hand, advanced technologies aggravate the environment situations.
Moreover, it has provoked some serious problems, which we will discuss now.

Mining For Minerals

A considerable number of finite natural resources and precious metals go into making our electronic devices and other modern technology. The picture is complex, and the ingredients required to make just one phone come from worldwide. This can make the end-to-end analysis of natural resource degradation difficult.

Electronic Waste

A small proportion of old electronic devices are recycled. However, the vast majority end up in landfill sites or burned in dumps. Sadly, these dumps emit methane and other carbon emissions, and our discarded devices leach chemicals, including mercury, that blend with other wastewater contaminants such as pesticides.

Carbon Footprint

The manufacturing process of electronic and technological products requires a significant amount of energy, which contributes to carbon emissions.This has led to an increase in greenhouse gases, which contribute to climate change. However, renewable resources such as solar, geothermal, and wind can help reduce the carbon footprint of energy production.

Climate change

Data centers, which store and process digital information, require substantial amounts of electricity. The energy demand for these facilities, along with the manufacturing and use of electronic devices, contributes to carbon emissions, exacerbating climate change. Efforts to improve energy efficiency in IT and transition to renewable energy sources are essential to mitigate these impacts.

Global warming

Global warming, driven by the accumulation of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane in the atmosphere, is exacerbated by various human activities, including those related to information technology. The production and usage of electronic devices contribute to carbon emissions.

Massive energy usage

Massive energy usage poses a significant threat to our environment. The burning of fossil fuels to generate electricity and power our homes, businesses, and vehicles releases greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat, leading to global warming, climate change, and a range of other environmental problems.
